39.5

Possible answers:

1 something totally covered by fire or water
2 something going down very fast, often into water
3 something totally covered – a shroud is the cloth that traditionally covers a dead body
4 a battle (The last ditch is the last part of an army’s defences, i.e. the last point at which it can avoid defeat.)
5 something being saved in the last few moments before disaster strikes (at 12 o’clock)
6 a country divided into pieces by war.

They are all dramatic images, which is why journalists like them.
