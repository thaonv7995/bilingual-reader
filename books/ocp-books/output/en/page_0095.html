In OO development, there is often a need to model an entity such that it would be accessible to all but extensible only by a selected few. In other words, you want a restricted hierarchy of classes. For example, let’s say you are developing a library for valuation of financial instruments such as stocks and bonds. You want to be able to refer to all financial instruments using a common base class so that you could invoke methods that are common to all financial instruments without worrying about the exact instrument. However, as the author of the library, you also know that some functionality of your library, or to put it in concrete terms, some methods in your library, cannot deal will all kinds of financial instruments. In fact, you know that many methods of your library can deal with only two kinds of financial instruments — Stock and Bond.
