Until we call t.start(), there is only one thread in execution, i.e., the main thread. The call to t.start() launches a new thread concurrently with the main thread. From our perspective, both the threads execute independently from each other from this point on. Under the hood, the OS gets both the threads executed as and when a CPU becomes available to execute their instructions.
