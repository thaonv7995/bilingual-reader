The output illustrates the following points about the between method:

  1. The difference between two date time objects is computed in the same unit as the ChronoUnit constant on which the between method is invoked. That is why ChronoUnits.MINUTES.between converts the difference of 16 hours 43 minutes to 1003 minutes.
  2. It returns "second argument" minus "first argument". That is why the the returned value is negative 1003.
  3. The difference ignores/truncates the parts of the date/time that are less significant than the unit used. That is why the 58 seconds part was ignored from the calculation when the unit was MINUTES and the 43 minutes and 58 seconds part was ignored when the unit was HOURS.

The between method can be used to compare two different types of date/time objects but there is a catch. If the the types of the two objects are different, the second type is translated to an instance of the first type before the calculating the difference.
