The catch(FileNotFoundException | ArrayIndexOutOfBoundsException e) clause is called a multi-catch clause because it allows you to catch multiple exceptions in the same catch clause. Although this feature is not used very often in real life, you may see a question or two on it in the exam. So, let us check out the rules for this clause. There are only two of them really.

  1. Any number of classes may be listed in a multi-catch clause but none of them may have an ancestor/successor relationship between them. For example, you cannot have catch(NullPointerException|Exception e) because Exception is an ancestor class (a grandparent, actually) of NullPointerException . This restriction makes sense because if a catch clause can catch Exception , it will catch subclasses of Exception as well.
