There are other overloaded variants of the now method that work on the same principle. For example, you may create a ZonedDateTime that represents the current time in another time zone using ZonedDateTime.now(ZoneId zid). So, if I call ZonedDateTime.now(ZoneId.of("America/New_York")) when my system time shows 26th Apr 2024 9PM IST, it will return the date and time representing 26th Apr 2024 11.30AM EST because US/Eastern time zone is 9.30 hours behind IST. (IST is 5.30 hours ahead of UTC and EST is 4 hours behind UTC, so, EST is 9.30 hours behind IST)

Since Period and Duration denote an amount of time and not a point in time, they don’t have the now() method. But they do have a static constant named ZERO, which denotes a period/duration of zero amount.
