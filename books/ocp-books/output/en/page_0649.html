Translating from ZonedDateTime and OffsetDateTime to Instant

Since an Instant corresponds to a time in UTC (which has 0 offset), translating a ZonedDateTime or a OffsetDateTime to an Instant is possible and just requires the offset amount of these types to be subtracted from the time that they contain. For example, a ZonedDateTime of 2024-12-01T06:00-05:00[America/New_York] will translate to an Instant representing 2024-12-01T11:00Z (because 6 - -5 = 11). Similarly, a ZonedDateTime of 2024-04-29T19:30+05:30[Asia/Calcutta]will translate to an Instant representing 2024-04-29T14:00:00Z (because 19:30 - 5:30 = 14:00).

However, an Instant cannot be translated to a ZonedDateTime or an OffsetDateTime because it wouldn’t know what zone id or offset amount to use for the translation. Of course, you can create them from an Instant by providing a ZoneId or a ZoneOffset. For example: instant.atZone(ZoneId.of("America/New_York") or instant.atOffset(ZoneOffset.ofHoursMinutes(1, 20)).
