Probability and luck

A Probability

YOUR WEEK IN THE STARS

You try something on the off-chance1 at work this week and achieve success against all the odds2. At home you must be careful not to take someone close to you for granted3. If you do, it is a foregone conclusion4 that you will regret it sooner rather than later. Others have seen changes in your love life coming a mile off5 and it is touch-and-go6 whether your

current relationship will survive this week's problems. However, you may still be able to get back together. Do not listen to others when they say that the cards are stacked against7 you. It is always worth chancing your arm⁸. Whether you succeed or not is in the lap of the gods9.

  • because you hope you will succeed or find something or someone even if you do not think success is very likely
  • despite all the problems making it unlikely that you will succeed
  • to fail to show you're grateful to someone because they are with you so often
  • a result obvious to everyone even before it happens
  • have seen that something, usually something bad, is likely to happen ('a mile' adds emphasis and is not always included in the expression)
  • not certain (often followed by whether)
  • you are not likely to succeed because there are so many obstacles in your way
  • taking a risk to achieve what you want (informal)
  • is not something that you can control yourself

B Luck

Possible stimulus You say Meaning
I got away with missing some school last Friday, so I'm going to try taking all of Friday off next week. Don't push your luck! Don't try too hard to get what you want and risk losing what you have.
Would you like to choose which hotel room you want? We'll take pot luck. We'll take whatever's available without knowing if it will be good or bad.
Do you always get a good horse at the riding stables you go to? It's the luck of the draw. It's a matter of chance and you have no control over it (draw = competition where winners are chosen at random).
You're playing against the best player in the competition! Just my luck! You say this, usually humorously, to mean that you are always unlucky.
Did you manage to persuade Laura to come on holiday with you? No such luck! You are disappointed you weren't able to do what you would have liked to do.
I dreamt that Justin Timberlake invited me to one of his parties last night. You should be so lucky! What someone wants to happen is very unlikely to happen.
