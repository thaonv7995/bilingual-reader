Lateral Joins

LATERAL is a new ANSI SQL construction in version 9.3. Here’s the motivation behind it: suppose you perform joins on two tables or subqueries; normally, the pair partici­pating in the join are independent units and can’t read data from each other. LATERAL lets you share data in columns across two tables in a FROM clause. However, it works only in one direction: the righthand side can draw from the left side, but not vice versa.
