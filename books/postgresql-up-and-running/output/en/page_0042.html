Roles

PostgreSQL represents accounts as roles. Roles that can log in are called login roles. Roles can be members of other roles; roles that contain other roles are called group roles. (And yes, group roles can be members of other group roles and so on ad infinitum, but don’t go there unless you have a knack for hierarchical thinking.) Roles that are group and can log in are called group login roles. However, for easier maintainability and security, DBAs generally don’t grant login rights to group roles. A role can be designated as superuser. Superuser roles have unfettered access to the PostgreSQL service.
